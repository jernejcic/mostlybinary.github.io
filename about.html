Chime Squirrel

About

The key to Chime Squirrel is simplicity

Instead of a ton of functionality in the app and trying to keep you in it, I hope that it gives you the ability to do something else. I see it's use as being similar to the way a person uses an egg timer.

Origins

This app was developed out of a personal need. Due to some back problems (probably from sitting in front of a computer so much, ironically) I had been seeing a chiropractor. One of the things he immediately had me do was daily stretches: first thing in the morning and last thing at night. Originally I would keep an eye on the clock or a watch, but soon I found myself making better use of the time (or just easily distracted) and needed something a little more automated, something that would allow my focus to be elsewhere then counting the seconds. And That is where the idea for Chime Squirrel came from.

Version 1.0

This first version was about the basics. It brought the Quick Chime functionality. It served as a quick way to setup a repeating chirp.

Version 2.0

After consistently using the first version, I saw opportunity to expand it. I wanted to be able to plan out intervals, for things like making coffee in my french press or possibly even for my C25K workout. It wasn't a completely rewrite since I didn't rewrite the Quick Chime functionality. However, the new interval functionality was much more work then the first version, probably four times the work.

I hope that this app has helped you to be more productive / healthier / informed / etc.
